Stephen King is an American writer, producer and screenwriter. Best known as a writer, his books have reached a total of 350 million sales in 40 countries. More than 30 books have been adapted for films in 40 years of career. Currently, he is the ninth most translated author in the world.

Stephen King's Timeline

1947

Stephen Edwin King is born on Maine. The youngest son of Donald Edwin King and Ruth Pillsbury Nellie.

When King had only two years old, his father abandoned his family, leaving King and David, his adoptive brother, under the care of Ruth.

1949
1958

With serious financial problems, the family moved to Durham when he was eleven years old.

At school, he wrote stories based on the movies he watched and copied them with the help of his brother David. King sold them to friends, but his teachers disapproved and forced him to stop.

1960
1966

Enters the university of maine to study English, where he wrote a column titled "King's Garbage Truck" for the student newspaper, Maine Campus.

He graduates and marries tabitha spruce. He got a steady job as a teacher and began writing stories for various magazines. Most often, the stories helped in rent, since Stephen and Tabitha were lower middle class workers.

Three years later Steve outlined a story about a girl with psychic problems, but he did not like the result and threw it in the trash. Tabitha took everything back and encouraged Steve to conclude the story and send to a publisher, in her opinion it was a good story. Titled as "Carrie", the novel was accepted for publication by Doubleday.

King's mother, Ruth King, dies.

After the publication of the book and the early death of his mother, Steve and his family decide to move to Colorado, where he wrote the novel "The Shining" (published in 1977).

Still on this year, the family returned to Maine, where the writer finished "The Stand" (published in 1978) and maintained its permanent residence in Maine ever since.
